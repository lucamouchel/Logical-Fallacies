Given a topic, a stance and a set of 2 arguments, you are asked to evaluate the quality of these arguments by selecting the best one, or qualifying them as equally good or equally bad.

An example would be:

  • Topic: There should be no age restrictions on cosmetic surgery.
  • Stance: Supporting.
  • Argument 1: The American Medical Association recommends age restrictions on cosmetic procedures be lifted.
  • Argument 2: Cosmetics surgery is safe.

With this example, we can better define all elements involved:

  1. A Topic refers to the subject or issue that is being discussed. It is the focal point around which the argument revolves,
  2. The stance refers to a position or perspective that an individual takes regarding a particular topic. It represents their viewpoint, beliefs, or opinions on the subject matter being discussed. In our case, the stance will be either supporting or counter
  3. The argument is a reasoned or logical presentation of ideas or opinions aimed at persuading others or establishing a particular viewpoint as valid or true. It typically involves the exchange of assertions, evidence, and reasoning to support a claim or position.

Your task is to choose which, out of these different arguments, you consider to be more relevant, with respect to a given topic and a stance, taking three criteria into account:

  1. Your preferred argument properly addresses the matter in the topic.
  2. Your preferred argument corresponds to the given stance.
  3. ANY OTHER?

However, if you think all the arguments are equally good, or equally bad, you may select All are equally good or All are equally bad. If none are relevant enough, you may select None are relevant.

Important notes to consider:

Note, there are many other considerations, but these are the most important.

  • You must pick not only the most qualitative argument but also the most relevant. An argument on its own can be good or bad, but what you are evaluating is how relevant the argument is with respect to the topic and how well this argument adresses the topic. Please check the examples provided below!
  • Please think about each example carefully and do your best to avoid any gender, racial, religious, sexual orientation or any other bias while evaluating the arguments.
Example 1:

Topic: Gender neutral language would not be good in every situation.

Stance: Supporting

Argument #1: Gender neutral language can be a confusing concept to understand.

Argument #2: Gender neutral language is not good because it would not be good in every situation.

Gold answer: Argument #1 is better

Example 2:

Topic: Companies should be allowed to decide workers' pay.

Stance: Counter

Argument #1: Companies abused wage autonomy until a federal minimum wage was instituted.

Argument #2: It is not fair for companies to keep profits and deny workers a fair wage.

Gold answer: Argument #2 is better

Example 3:

Topic: Stem cell research should be banned.

Stance: Counter

Argument #1: Stem cell research provides breakthroughs.

Argument #2: Those who support stem cell research are heartless and lack moral values.

Gold answer: Argument #1 is better
